# Attention Fatigue and Restoration Design

Attention fatigue emerges when executive control systems are continuously taxed without meaningful recovery cycles. The result is reduced working memory precision, slower error correction, and emotional reactivity.

## Observable signs

- Frequent task-switching without completion.
- Increased rereading with low comprehension.
- Irritation toward low-stakes interruptions.

## Restoration model

Instead of relying on motivation spikes, design structured recovery:

- 25 minutes focused work.
- 5 minutes visual distance + posture reset.
- 2 minutes breath tracking.
- 1 sentence checkpoint: “What is the next single action?”

## Neural perspective

Recovery intervals likely help normalize network competition between task-positive systems and internally directed mind-wandering dynamics. In practical terms: short resets reduce cognitive drift.

## Implementation advice

Measure completion quality over 14 days before and after adopting recovery cycles. The expected gain is not only productivity, but cleaner subjective clarity and lower cognitive friction.
