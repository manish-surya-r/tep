# Advaita Vedanta and the Observer Problem

Advaita Vedanta proposes that the apparent subject-object split is provisional, not absolute. In modern scientific language, this can be interpreted as a challenge to rigid self-model assumptions.

## Philosophical-to-computational bridge

In many cognitive models, “self” is a dynamic control process: a prediction-and-action hub that stabilizes behavior. Advaita asks whether this hub is the final observer—or simply another process appearing in awareness.

This distinction is productive for neuroscience because it separates:

- **Operational self-models** (needed for action and memory), and
- **Phenomenal awareness** (the field in which models appear).

## Research relevance

When participants are trained in introspective discrimination (e.g., observing thought as object rather than identity), they may produce cleaner reports in tasks on agency, ownership, and metacognition.

## Suggested inquiry exercise

During reflective journaling, separate each line into:

- “Event observed”
- “Interpretation generated”
- “Identity claim attached”

This simple structure reveals how often cognition fuses data with narrative ownership.
