# Yoga Nidra and Memory Reprocessing

Yoga Nidra creates a guided transition through layered states of awareness—awake but deeply relaxed. This state may support memory reprocessing by reducing cognitive interference and defensive rumination.

## Why it may work

When physiological arousal decreases, emotional charge around specific memories often drops. That may open a window for reconsolidation: old memory traces updating with new emotional context.

## Practice framework

- 20–30 minutes session.
- Body scan to stabilize interoceptive attention.
- Breath counting to reduce cognitive chatter.
- Brief intention setting (one sentence only).

## Research opportunity

Track pre/post session values for:

- Emotional intensity of target memory.
- Intrusive thought frequency.
- Sleep onset latency.

Across weeks, this can generate meaningful self-study data on memory and regulation.

Yoga Nidra is an ideal bridge modality for your project: rooted in Indian contemplative tradition, measurable with modern cognitive and affective metrics.
