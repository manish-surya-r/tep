# Prediction Error Minimization in Daily Cognition

Prediction error is the difference between expected and observed input. Brains learn by minimizing this error—but not by eliminating uncertainty entirely. Healthy cognition balances stability and adaptive revision.

## Everyday examples

- Social misread corrected by feedback.
- Motor adjustment during skill practice.
- Belief update after surprising evidence.

## Practical method

At the end of each day, log three moments:

1. What did I expect?
2. What actually happened?
3. What model update follows?

This turns abstract theory into cognitive training.

## Risk of overfitting

If we update too quickly, we become unstable and overly reactive. If we update too slowly, we become rigid. The art is weighted updating based on context quality and evidence strength.

## Why this matters for consciousness work

Tracking prediction error in daily life reveals how perception, identity, and emotion are co-constructed. It also creates a grounded path from computational theory to introspective practice.
