# Predictive Processing and Intuition

Intuition is often treated as either mystical certainty or irrational guesswork. A more useful framing is that intuition may be a high-speed output from learned predictive models that operate below deliberate verbal reasoning.

## Mechanistic view

The brain continuously generates predictions about incoming sensory and social data. When a pattern has been encoded deeply through repeated exposure, the system can issue a confidence signal before explicit explanation is available.

That “felt sense” may be intuition.

## Calibration matters

Uncalibrated intuition can be biased and misleading. Calibrated intuition is measured against outcomes.

Use a simple loop:

- Record intuition statement.
- Assign confidence score (0–100).
- Log eventual outcome.
- Review calibration monthly.

## Why this helps research

It converts subjective intuitions into trackable variables. You can ask:

- Which contexts produce reliable intuition?
- Which emotional states degrade it?
- How does sleep, stress, or meditation influence accuracy?

The Entanglement Project can treat intuition as an experimentally testable phenomenon: introspective signal + behavioral verification + computational analysis.
